As a fresher, you often compete with hundreds of applicants for the same role. Your resume must show **potential, skills, and clarity** — even without years of full-time experience.

## Ideal length

- **1 page** for most freshers
- **2 pages** only if you have significant internships, projects, and certifications

Recruiters spend seconds on the first scan. Make every line count.

## Recommended sections (in order)

1. **Contact information** — name, phone, email, city, LinkedIn (optional)
2. **Career objective or summary** — 2–3 lines targeting the role
3. **Education** — degree, college, year, CGPA (if strong)
4. **Skills** — technical and soft skills, grouped clearly
5. **Projects / Academic projects** — most important for tech roles
6. **Internships** — if any
7. **Certifications & achievements** — hackathons, competitions, scholarships
8. **Extracurriculars** — leadership roles, volunteer work (brief)

## What to emphasize without work experience

### Projects

Treat projects like work experience. For each project include:

- Problem you solved
- Technologies used
- Your specific contribution
- Outcome or link (GitHub, live demo)

**Example:**

> **E-commerce Inventory Dashboard** — Built a React + Node.js dashboard for a college mini-project. Implemented stock alerts and sales charts; reduced manual tracking time for the demo store by ~50%.

### Internships

Even a 1–2 month internship deserves 3–4 bullet points with measurable outcomes.

### Skills

List skills you can **discuss in an interview**. Group as:

- **Programming:** Python, Java, SQL
- **Tools:** Git, Excel, Figma
- **Soft skills:** Communication, teamwork, problem-solving

## Formatting tips for Indian recruiters

- Use a **professional email** (not nicknames)
- Include **city/location** — many roles are hybrid or on-site
- CGPA: include if 7.5+ (or as per your college norm); otherwise optional
- Avoid photo unless the employer specifically asks (common in some sectors)
- Use **PDF** when submitting online

## Objective vs summary

**Objective** (role-specific):

> B.Tech CSE graduate seeking an entry-level software developer role. Strong in Python, data structures, and REST APIs through academic projects and a 3-month internship.

**Summary** (broader):

> Detail-oriented graduate with hands-on project experience in web development and data analysis. Quick learner with internship exposure to Agile teams and client deliverables.

Pick one — not both.

## Common fresher mistakes

1. Long paragraphs instead of bullet points
2. Listing every subject from college
3. Generic objective copied from the internet
4. No projects for IT/engineering roles
5. Spelling errors in skills (e.g. "Javscript")

## ATS tip for campus placements

Many campus drives use ATS or Excel uploads. Use standard headings (**Education**, **Skills**, **Projects**) and avoid complex templates.
